Research Interests

CAC is a term I made up, standing for Computer Aided CAC. As computational tools for design have evolved, they’ve become specialized for specific applications, creating gaps between them. Recently, I’ve been exploring ways to address this by rethinking the design process, framing it as a cross-domain tool where computers ultimately aid themselves. In this sense, CAC can be understood as the "glue" between shape (CAD), analysis (CAE), and fabrication (CAM).

Why - Algorithmic Design: An approach where the design is determined by the method, and the method is shaped by the design requirements.

How - Geometry Processing: Describing surfaces by solving PDEs on them, including the study of diffusion-reaction equations.

What - Digital Fabrication: Developing workflows that strengthen the connection between computational models and real-world objects.
